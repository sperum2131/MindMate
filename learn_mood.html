Boosting Mood

Find out how mood works, what influences it, and discover evidence-based ways to boost your mood and emotional well-being.

What is Mood?

Mood is your general emotional state. It is how you feel over a period of time, not just in a single moment. Unlike quick-changing emotions, mood tends to last for hours or even days. Your mood can be positive (like feeling cheerful or content) or negative (like feeling down or irritable), and it can shift for many reasons.

Everyone experiences ups and downs in mood. These changes are a normal part of life and can be influenced by things like sleep, stress, physical health, relationships, environment, and even what you eat or how much you move.

What Influences Mood?

Many factors can affect your mood, including:

  • Sleep: Not getting enough rest can make you feel irritable, sad, or anxious.
  • Physical activity: Moving your body releases chemicals that can boost your mood and energy.
  • Nutrition: Eating regular, balanced meals helps keep your energy and mood steady.
  • Stress: Ongoing stress can lower your mood and make it harder to cope with daily life.
  • Social life: Spending time with friends, family, or pets can lift your spirits.
  • Thought patterns: Negative or self-critical thoughts can bring your mood down, while positive self-talk can help you feel better.
  • Environment: Sunlight, fresh air, and a comfortable space can all influence how you feel.

Ways to Boost Your Mood

While you can't control everything that affects your mood, there are simple, evidence-based steps you can take to help lift your spirits:

  • Get moving: Even a short walk, stretching, or dancing to music can help release feel-good chemicals in your brain.
  • Connect with others: Reach out to a friend, family member, or support group—even a quick chat can make a difference.
  • Practice gratitude: Take a moment to notice and appreciate something good, no matter how small.
  • Spend time outside: Sunlight and nature can help improve your mood and reduce stress.
  • Do something you enjoy: Make time for hobbies, music, art, or anything that brings you joy or relaxation.
  • Try mindfulness or relaxation: Deep breathing, meditation, or simply focusing on the present can help calm your mind.
  • Take care of your body: Eat regular meals, stay hydrated, and aim for enough sleep each night.
  • Be kind to yourself: It's okay to have low days. Treat yourself with the same compassion you'd offer a friend.
  • Ask for help: If your mood stays low for more than a couple of weeks, or if it's hard to cope, consider reaching out to a mental health professional.

Remember, everyone has ups and downs. Small steps—like moving your body, connecting with others, or taking a mindful moment—can add up and help you feel better over time.
